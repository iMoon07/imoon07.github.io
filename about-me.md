Hello! I'm Raja Muhammad Kurnia Setyawan. At 21, I began my career as a **self-taught Penetration Tester** with a strong focus on *Offensive Security*.

## Origin Story

My fascination with technology started in elementary school. Growing up as an "internet cafe kid," I loved exploring games and using cheats—purely out of curiosity to see how a system's rules and boundaries could be bypassed. This curiosity drove me to major in Computer and Network Engineering (TKJ) at SMKN 1 Bintan Utara.

My tinkering instincts sharpened when I started self-learning server management alongside the **Growtopia Private Server (GTPS)** community. For context, Growtopia is a popular 2D sandbox MMO known for its economy and building mechanics. Meanwhile, GTPS refers to independent private servers built from leaked source codes. Being involved in this community gave me a free playground to experiment with client-server networking, game logic modification (often involving C++), and full server administration.

Later, at the age of 20, I finally got my first "battle machine" computer (12 vCPU, 16GB RAM, RTX 3060, 500GB SSD). Armed with this machine, my exploration exploded. I actively consumed various bootcamp programs—starting with **Merdeka Siber**, followed by a second bootcamp at **[Cyber Sentinel Secure](https://cybersentinelsecure.asia/)**.

Equipped with knowledge from these intensive trainings, I secured a valuable opportunity for my first internship at **PT. Teknologi Server Indonesia** ([xcode.co.id](https://xcode.co.id/)). There, I practiced fundamental hacking and cybersecurity under the guidance of senior practitioners. This series of internship and bootcamp experiences proved to be a strong catalyst, eventually landing my first professional role as a *Freelance Junior Pentester* at [Merdeka Siber](https://merdekasiber.com).

This journey has just begun. At 21, the space to keep exploring is still vast, and I will continue to grow.
